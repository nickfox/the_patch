Abstract\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 While natural language is the de facto com-\

\fs20\fsmilli10027 munication medium for LLM-based agents, it\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 presents a fundamental constraint. The pro-\

\fs19\fsmilli9863 cess of downsampling rich, internal latent states\

\fs19\fsmilli9948 into discrete tokens inherently limits the depth\

\fs19\fsmilli9863 and nuance of information that can be transmit-\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 ted, thereby hindering collaborative problem-\

\fs19\fsmilli9923 solving. Inspired by telepathy, which bypasses\

\fs19\fsmilli9978 symbolic language in communication, we pro-\

\fs20\fsmilli10017 pose Interlat (
\fs19\fsmilli9963 Inter
\fs20\fsmilli10017 -agent 
\fs19\fsmilli9963 Lat
\fs20\fsmilli10017 ent Space Com-\

\fs19\fsmilli9863 munication), a paradigm that leverages the con-\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 tinuous last hidden states of an LLM as a rep-\
resentation of its thought for direct commu-\
nication (termed \'93latent communication\'94).\

\fs19\fsmilli9893 An additional learned compression process fur-\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 ther compresses latent communication via la-\

\fs19\fsmilli9863 tent space reasoning. Experiments demonstrate\

\fs19\fsmilli9908 that Interlat outperforms both fine-tuned chain-\

\fs20\fsmilli10057 of-thought (CoT) prompting and single-agent\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 baselines, even across heterogeneous models,\
promoting more exploratory behavior and en-\

\fs19\fsmilli9868 abling genuine utilization of latent information.\

\fs19\fsmilli9988 Further compression not only substantially ac-\

\fs19\fsmilli9863 celerates inference by up to 24
\fs19\fsmilli9963 \'d7
\fs19\fsmilli9863 but also main-\

\fs19\fsmilli9988 tains competitive performance through an effi-\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 cient information-preserving mechanism. We\
position this work as a feasibility study of en-\

\fs20\fsmilli10042 tirely latent space inter-agent communication,\

\fs20\fsmilli10062 and our results highlight its potential, offer-\
ing valuable insights for future research. Our\

\fs23\fsmilli11955 1 Introduction\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 Large language model (LLM)-based agentic sys-\
tems have emerged as a promising paradigm for\
solving complex tasks by orchestrating multi-\
ple agents through natural language communica-\

\fs21\fsmilli10876 tion (\cf3 Wang et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2025\cf2 , \cf3 2024\cf2 ; \cf3 Zhang et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2024b\cf2 ;\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 \cf3 Tran et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2025\cf2 ). Despite its human readability,\

\fs21\fsmilli10915 natural language imposes fundamental constraints\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 on inter-agent communication. To communicate,\

\fs21\fsmilli10887 an agent must compress its rich, high-dimensional\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 internal states into a sequence of discrete tokens,\
typically exposing only a single linear message\
(i.e., a chain of thought (CoT) (\cf3 Wei et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2022\cf2 )\

\fs21\fsmilli10980 plan). This downsampling not only discards alter-\

\fs21\fsmilli10985 native reasoning paths, but also incurs substantial\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 redundancy, as much of the generated text serves\

\fs21\fsmilli10833 linguistic coherence rather than task-relevant infor-\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 mation (\cf3 Zhang et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2024a\cf2 ). As a result, language-\

\fs21\fsmilli10833 based communication can be ambiguous and lossy,\

\fs21\fsmilli10969 which has been identified as a major source of co-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 ordination failures in multi-agent systems (\cf3 Chen\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2025\cf2 ; \cf3 Cemri et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2025\cf2 ).\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 To move beyond language space, we explore the\

\fs22\fsmilli11007 direct transmission of internal representations for\

\fs21\fsmilli10975 more precise and information-preserving commu-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 nication. In multi-agent settings, we refer to this\

\fs21\fsmilli10985 as latent communication. While direct sharing is\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 challenging for humans, which is often depicted\

\fs21\fsmilli10947 in fictions (\cf3 Liu\cf2 , \cf3 2008\cf2 ), i.e., telepathy, LLM-based\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 agents naturally perform most of their computa-\

\fs21\fsmilli10931 tion in latent space and produce rich hidden states\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 throughout generation, which can be extracted to\
support direct, expressive communication. Previ-\
ous hidden-state-based communication methods\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 either rely on one-shot activation grafting (\cf3 Ramesh\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 and Li\cf2 , \cf3 2025\cf2 ) or remain coupled to language tra-\

\fs21\fsmilli10985 jectories (\cf3 Tang et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2025\cf2 ), and typically require\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 ad-hoc layer selection, adding tuning overhead.\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 In this work, we propose Interlat, a novel\
framework that realizes this vision by enabling\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 1
\fs24 \cf0 \page 
\fs21\fsmilli10871 \cf2 inter-agent communication entirely in latent space.\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 Rather than transmitting discrete tokens decoded\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 by the language head, Interlat directly transmits the\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 temporally aligned last-layer hidden states corre-\

\fs21\fsmilli10822 sponding to an agent\'92s generated message, treating\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 them as a representation of its thoughts. Under\

\fs21\fsmilli10898 this formulation, we design a supervised objective\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 that explicitly encourages the interpretation and\

\fs21\fsmilli10849 utilization of task-relevant latent information, with\

\fs21\fsmilli10942 a simple but effective stochastic token\'96latent mix-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 ing curriculum to stabilize training. To overcome\
the rigidity of full-trajectory message communi-\
cation while preserving information integrity, we\
further train a separate reasoning process that au-\

\fs21\fsmilli10996 toregressively generates compact latent messages\

\fs21\fsmilli10980 with a controllable number of generation steps di-\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 rectly in latent space, without decoding to language\
space tokens. This allows Interlat to compress long\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 reasoning trajectories into concise latent prefixes,\

\fs21\fsmilli10942 substantially improving efficiency while retaining\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 task-critical information for downstream execution.\
Experimentally, we focus on a two-agent sender-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 receiver scenario, which is the building block of\

\fs21\fsmilli10947 various multi-agent systems. To reduce confound-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 ing factors, we intentionally exclude components\
such as tool use, retrieval, or multi-round debate.\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 Analysis reveals that agents utilizing latent commu-\
nication exhibit more exploratory behavior patterns\

\fs21\fsmilli10811 that lead to higher success rates by leveraging task-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 relevant latent information rather than superficial\

\fs21\fsmilli10991 pattern matching. Moreover, we demonstrate that\

\fs22\fsmilli11002 latent messages can be compressed to as few as 8\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 tokens while maintaining competitive performance,\
achieving up to a 
\fs21\fsmilli10909 24\'d7
\fs21\fsmilli10800 reduction in communication\

\fs21\fsmilli10985 latency. Further analysis of the output probability\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 distribution after compression reveals how task-\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 critical information is effectively preserved.\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 In this work, we introduced Interlat, a paradigm\

\fs21\fsmilli10876 that enables inter-agent communication entirely in\

\fs21\fsmilli10871 latent space. Across experiments, our results show\

\fs21\fsmilli10855 that directly transmitting and reasoning over latent\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 states improves task performance and achieves sub-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 stantially higher communication efficiency, even\

\fs21\fsmilli10887 demonstrating compatibility across heterogeneous\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 models. Beyond full-length latent exchange, we\
show that latent communication can be aggres-\

\fs21\fsmilli10920 sively compressed through latent-space reasoning,\

\fs21\fsmilli10871 forming a compact, task-preserving representation\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 that retains parallel hypotheses while discarding\
redundant structure. Together, these findings sug-\
gest that communication need not be bound to\

\fs21\fsmilli10980 language tokens, highlighting latent states as a vi-\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 able, efficient, and generalizable medium for next-\
generation multi-agent systems.\

C.2 Baselines and settings in Interlat.\

\fs21\fsmilli10969 We consider two external baselines, which do not rely on latent communication at all. All baselines are\

\fs21\fsmilli10898 trained using the same base models (Qwen2.5-7B/0.5B-Base, LLaMA3.1-8B-Base) as Interlat, differing\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 only in whether and how inter-agent communication is provided.\
1. 
\fs22\fsmilli11018 CoT (full). We use complete Chain-of-Thought (CoT) traces produced by a related instruction-\

\fs21\fsmilli10805 tuned model (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct, and LLaMA3.1-8B-Instruct) to perform\

\fs21\fsmilli10904 full-parameter supervised fine-tuning. In inference, the model receives a complete CoT plan before\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 generating answers.\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 Rationale. This baseline serves as a strong upper bound for language-based communication: it\

\fs21\fsmilli10844 evaluates whether latent communication can surpass explicit human-readable planning, and controls\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 for the supervision quality provided by an instruction-tuned teacher.\
2. 
\fs21\fsmilli10931 No-CoT. The language model is trained to produce the final answer directly, without receiving any\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 plan from other agents.\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 Rationale. This baseline isolates the contribution of any communication signal. It tests whether\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 inter-agent exchange, latent or linguistic, is necessary for solving multi-step tasks.\

\fs23\fsmilli11955 D Benchmark\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 D.1 Alfworld\

\fs21\fsmilli10865 Alfworld (\cf3 Shridhar et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2020\cf2 ) is a text-only benchmark that simulates embodied household tasks while\

\fs21\fsmilli10827 keeping interaction purely in natural language. Agents observe textual descriptions of the scene and issue\

\fs21\fsmilli10876 high-level commands from a constrained action set (e.g., go to, open, close, take, put, toggle on/off, heat,\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 16
\fs24 \cf0 \page 
\fs21\fsmilli10860 \cf2 cool, examine). Tasks are long-horizon and compositional, requiring perception, planning, and execution\

\fs21\fsmilli10936 over multiple steps under partial observability. The benchmark provides official train/seen/unseen splits\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 and a standard success metric under a fixed step budget (e.g., 20 steps in our setup), enabling systematic\
and reproducible evaluation of sequential decision-making.\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 Task Setup and Evaluation Metrics. ALFWorld (\cf3 Shridhar et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2020\cf2 ) is a text-based embodied\

\fs21\fsmilli10882 reasoning benchmark where an agent must interact with a simulated household environment to complete\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 goal-oriented tasks (e.g., put the apple in the fridge). Each episode begins with a textual scene description,\

\fs21\fsmilli10855 and in our settings, 
\fs21\fsmilli10909 we allow up to 20 environment steps for an actor agent to solve a task. 
\fs21\fsmilli10855 At every step,\

\fs21\fsmilli10816 the agent observes the updated environment state and issues a textual command from a constrained action\

\fs21\fsmilli10887 set (go to, open, close, take, put, toggle on/off, heat, cool, examine, etc. ), receiving a textual observation\

\fs21\fsmilli10838 and reward signal. We train agents on trajectories derived from expert demonstrations (\cf3 Song et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2024\cf2 ),\

\fs21\fsmilli10811 which include the environment descriptions, intermediate thoughts, and executed actions. During training,\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 the actor agent predicts the next action conditioned on task context or received latent communication, while\

\fs21\fsmilli10936 the actor model executes and provides cross-entropy feedback. Alfworld evaluates agents\'92 performance\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 using two primary metrics: 
\fs21\fsmilli10909 success rate and steps
\fs22\fsmilli11018 . Success rate measures the proportion of tasks in\

\fs21\fsmilli10871 which the final goal state is reached within the allowed step budget (Success = 1 if goal achieved, else 0).\

\fs21\fsmilli10849 \'93Steps\'94 reports the average number of environment interactions, i.e., action\'96observation cycles\'97taken to\

\fs21\fsmilli10838 successfully complete or terminate a task, not rounds of inter-agent communication. Higher success rates\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 indicate better reasoning and coordination efficiency. We provide training templates in Appendix \cf3 J\cf2 .\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 Why ALFWorld for this work? First, its multi-step, plan-then-act structure closely matches our sender-\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 receiver setup and stresses the precise abilities our method targets: exploration quality, plan following,\
and coordination. Prior work shows that continuous latent reasoning is especially advantageous\
on planning-heavy tasks, latent representations preserve multiple candidate reasoning branches and\

\fs21\fsmilli10975 promote breadth-first search (BFS) dynamics (\cf3 Hao et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2024\cf2 ). We ask whether the same or similar\

\fs21\fsmilli10893 advantages hold when agents communicate in latent space; ALFWorld is an ideal testbed because its\

\fs22\fsmilli11007 tasks require long-horizon planning, where agents iteratively observe, form thoughts, and act based on\

\fs21\fsmilli10849 environment feedback. Second, the text-only interface isolates the communication modality itself, letting\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 us cleanly contrast language space vs. latent space communication without confounds from external\
tools or perception pipelines, thereby allowing us to probe what new properties latent communication\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 introduces for agent behavior. Third, community resources provide consistent task descriptions and action\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 trajectories, allowing us to derive both language baselines (e.g., a CoT plan) and latent representations\
from the same underlying data, reducing distribution shift. Finally, the moderate episode length and\

\fs21\fsmilli10800 standardized protocol make it feasible to average over multiple independent runs, yielding robust statistics\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 for ablations and compression analyses.\
D.2 MATH\

\fs21\fsmilli10915 MATH (\cf3 Hendrycks et al.\cf2 , \cf3 2021\cf2 ) is a standard benchmark for evaluating mathematical reasoning in large\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 language models. It consists of high-quality competition-style problems spanning algebra, geometry,\

\fs21\fsmilli10904 number theory, and combinatorics, each annotated with a difficulty level from 1 to 5. Unlike ALFWorld,\

\fs22\fsmilli11018 MATH is a non-interactive, single-turn reasoning task: models must solve each problem without envi-\

\fs21\fsmilli10975 ronmental feedback or intermediate observations, producing a final answer in one pass. We use MATH\

\fs21\fsmilli10925 to assess whether latent communication provides benefits beyond interactive settings, particularly under\

\fs21\fsmilli10909 increasing reasoning complexity as reflected by higher difficulty levels.\
